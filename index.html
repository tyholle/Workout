Block 1 Structure
Dan John's Mass Made Simple exactly. DB Incline replaces barbell bench throughout (shoulder). Same 6-exercise skeleton every session. Complex weight = military press limit — never put the bar down mid-complex.
Session order: Warm-Up → DB Incline → One-Arm KB Press → Bat Wings → Bird Dog → Complex → High-Rep Squat → Abs (Mon/Fri) → 20-min Yoga → 15-min Sauna.
2-3-5 Ladder: 2 reps → 20-sec rest → 3 reps → 20-sec rest → 5 reps = 1 cluster (10 reps). Do 2 clusters = 20 reps same weight.
Complex (no bar down): Row → Power Clean → Front Squat → Military Press → Back Squat → Good Morning. Military press dictates the weight for the whole complex.
Complex progression: Wk 1–2: 5 rounds × 3 reps · Wk 3–4: 4 rounds × 4 reps, add weight after rnd 3 · Wk 5–6: 3 rounds × 5 reps, push weight each round. Final weight = starting weight next session.
Squat targets: Wk 1: 2×30 @ 65 · Wk 2: 2×30 @ 75 · Wk 3: 2×40 @ 85 · Wk 4: 2×40 @ 95 · Wk 5: 1×50 @ 115 · Wk 6: 1×MAX @ 135 lbs.

Golf Athletic Days · Tue + Thu · Block 1
Built around TPI (Titleist Performance Institute) movement principles and research by Dr. Greg Rose. Rotational power, hip-shoulder separation, single-leg stability, and anti-rotation strength are the four proven pillars of athletic golf fitness. This session trains all four at low-to-moderate intensity so it doesn't compromise MMS recovery on M/W/F.
Order: Dynamic Warm-Up (5 min) → Power (chops, slams) → Stability (Pallof, SL-RDL) → Mobility (hip 90/90, thoracic) → Chest touch → Golf stretch (20 min). Rest 60–90 sec between sets.

Post-Session Yoga · MMS-Specific · ~20 Min
Designed for what MMS taxes. Heavy thoracic emphasis — the biggest limiting factor in this block. Do after every session while muscles are warm. Click any section to expand.
Thoracic note: Foam rolling unlocks it before you load it. This yoga reinforces it after. Both matter every session.
🧱1 · Thoracic Spine + Upper Back8 min ★▼
🌊
Cat-Cow (slow)
10 very slow reps post-session. Feel each vertebra move individually — not a warm-up, a deliberate mobilization.
10 reps
📖
Book Openers — Side-Lying Thoracic Rotation
Lie on side, knees at 90°. Sweep top arm overhead and behind, chest opens toward ceiling. Hold 2–3 sec at end range. Best active thoracic rotation drill there is.
8/side
🧵
Thread the Needle
On all fours, thread one arm under body along floor. Let shoulder touch ground, hold 30 sec. Releases rhomboids and mid-traps loaded by bat wings and rows.
30 sec/side
🪵
Foam Roller Thoracic Extension
Same as warm-up but go deeper now — muscles are warm. Extra time on spots that were tight during the session.
90 sec
✈️
Prone Y-T-W
Face down, chest slightly off floor. Arms through Y (overhead 45°), T (straight out), W (elbows bent, squeeze blades). Hold each 3 sec. Builds lower traps and serratus — capacity to stay extended, not just stretch.
8 each

Eagle Arms
Arms forward at shoulder height, wrap one arm under the other at elbows, lift. Stretches posterior rotator cuff and upper back.

Cross-Body Shoulder Stretch
